Scrubs & Journaling

[ January 1st, 2026 ]

It's easy for TV characters to be introspective, but conveying exactly what they're thinking without outright saying it? Not an easy feat. Writers are smart, and they sidestep this with actions -- no matter how subtle -- that bring the machinations of the subconscious mind to light. But Scrubs had a trick up its sleeve. Being a show that so freely delves into the mind of the main character, it got to show us exactly where J.D.'s thinking begins and ends. We get to see how he arrives at every life lesson, and how even the most mundane of things can have a huge impact on him.

At some point in my life, I decided I wanted to be a writer. Not necessarily as a job or anything -- though plans may have changed... for better or for worse -- but for my own fulfillment. I was enamored by the art of storytelling, something I never thought I could do but endlessly appreciated when anyone else did it. After contemplation, I decided this was a rather vague and intimidating goal. I figured I’d have to write something -- I’d later find out that it would be TV scripts -- despite not knowing where to begin with constructing a story. That wasn’t even the main issue. I had no idea how to even think like a writer.
